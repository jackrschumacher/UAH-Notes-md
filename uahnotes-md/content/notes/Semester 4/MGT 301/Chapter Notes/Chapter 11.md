### Responsibility and Job Descriptions
- **Responsibility**
	- Obligation to perform assigned activities
	- Source of responsibility lies within the individual
	- Managers remain responsible for tasks even if they have delegated them to another employee
- **Job description**
	- Summary of individual job activites
	- Communicates job content and performance expectations to employees

### Dividing Job Activities
- Functional similarity method
	- Examine management system objectives
	- Designate appropriate activities that must be performed
	- Design specific jobs by grouping similar activities
	- Make specific individuals responsible for performing the jobs
- Avoid overlapping responsibilities
- Avoid responsibility gaps
- Avoid creating activities that do not enhance goal attainment

- ### Clarifying Job Activities of Managers
  - **Management responsibility-** helps management describe responsibility relationships in the organization
  - **Responsible managers-** Managers deemed responsible when they perform activities they are obligated to perform

# Responsibility Relationships
- General responsibility
	- Directs the execution of the function through the person accepting operating responsibility
- Operating responsibility
	- Directly responsibly for execution
- Specific responsibility
	- Individual who is responsible for executing a specific or limited portion of function
- Must be consulted
	- Individual whose area is affected, must be consulted
- May be consulted
	- May be called on to relate information, render advice, make recommendations before action taken
- Must be notified
	- Individual who must be notified
- Must approve
	- Must approve/disapprove the decision

# Authority
- Right to perform or command
- Increases probability specific commands will be obeyed
- Does not always lead to obedience
- ## Acceptance of Authority
	- Individual accepts when
		- Individual can understand order being communicated
		- Individual believes the order is consistent with purpose of org
		- Sees the order as compatible with his or her personal interests
		- Individual is mentally and physically able to comply with order
	- Conditions that enhance acceptance of authority
		- Manager uses formal channels of communication that are familiar
		- Members have an assigned formal communication channel
		- Line of communication between manager and subordinate is direct
		- Complete chain of command is used to issue orders
		- Manager has communication skills
		- Formal communication lines only used for organizational business
		- Command is authenticated as coming from manager
- ## Types of authority
	- **Line authority**
		- Superior-subordinate relationships
	- **Staff authority**
		- Advise-assist those who possess line authority
	- **Functional authority**
		- Assigned to a segment that normally does not have authority
	- ![image.png](../assets/image_1772534494298_0.png)
- ### Roles of staff personnel
	- Advisory or counseling role
	- Service role
	- Control role

	- **Line personnel perspective**
		- Staff personnel assumes line authority

# Accountability
- **Employees liability to perform**
- **Nonperformance results in punishment**
- **Performance results in some kind of reward**
- ## Delegation
	- Assign specific duties to the individual
	- Grant authority to the individual
	- Create obligation
- ### Effective delegation
	- Give employees the freedom to pursue tasks in their own way.
	- Establish mutually agreed-upon results and performance standards for delegated tasks.
	- Encourage employees’ to take an active role in defining, implementing, and communicating
	  progress on tasks.  
	- Entrust employees with completion of whole projects or tasks whenever possible.
	- Explain the relevance of delegated tasks to larger projects or department or organization
	  goals.  
	- Give employees the authority necessary to accomplish tasks.
	- Allow employees access to all information, people, and departments necessary to perform delegated tasks.
	- Provide the training and guidance necessary for employees to complete delegated tasks satisfactorily.
	- When possible, delegate tasks on the basis of employee interests.
- ### Obstacles to delegation process
	- Supervisor
	- Subordinates
	- Organizations
- ### Eliminating obstacles to the delegation process
	- Find obstacles
	- Obstacles take time to overcome
	- Build subordinate confidence in use of delegated authority
	- Minimize the impact of delegation on established on established working relationships
	- Help Delegatee
- ### Centralization and Decentralization
	- **Centralization**
		- Minimal number of job activities and minimal amount of authority delegated to subordinates
	- **Decentralization**
		- Maximum amount of authority delegated to subordinates
