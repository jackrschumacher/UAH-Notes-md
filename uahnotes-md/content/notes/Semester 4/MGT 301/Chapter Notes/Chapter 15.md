- ## Leader vs Manager
	- ### Leader
		- Focuses on People
		- Inspires, motivates, influences behavior
		- Subset of management
		- Can exist without a formal title
		- Emphasizes behavioral issues
	- ### Manger
		- Broader Scope
		- Ensures the job gets done
		- Uses planning, organizing and controlling
		- Formal role and authority based
		- Emphasizes behavioral and nonbehavioral issues
- ## Trait Theory vs Behavioral Approach
	- ### Trait Theory
		- Leaders are born not made
		- Focus on who the leader is
		- Common traits: intelligence, emotional maturity, drive
		- Problem: No universal trait set identified
		- Inconsistent across studies → Largely rejected
	- ### Behavioral Approach
		- Focuses on what leaders do
		- Structure / Job centered: task focus
		- Consideration / Employee centered focus
		- Leaders can learn and develop those behaviors

- ## Life Cycle Theroy
	- Core idea
	- Low Maturity → High Task, Low Relationship
	- Moderate Maturity → High Task + High Relationship
	- Moderate-High Maturity → Low Task, High Relationship
	- High Maturity → Low Task, Low Relationship
- ## Contingency Theory
	- Key Insight
	- Three factors determine situational fit
	- Task oriented leaders work best in extreme situations
	- Relationship-oriented leaders work best in middle ground
	- Common exam mistake

- ### Vroom-Yetton-Jago Model
	- Puprose
	- Two goals every decision must meet
		- High quality outcome
		- Subordinate acceptance/commitment to the decision
	- 5 Decision styles (AI-GII)
	- Pick a style by working through the style
	- Decision making participation
- ### Transformational Leadership
	- ### What it is
		- Inspires organizational success by affecting followers beliefs and values
		- Closely related to charismatic and inspiration leadership
		- Raises follower awareness of organizational issues
		- Creates a compelling vision
		- Builds commitment and facilities organizational change
	- ### Key Characteristics
		- Vision - paints a clear, desirable future state
		- Articulation - communicates vision persuasively
		- Trust - Followers believe in the leaders
		- Risk - leader takes personal risks to pursue the vision
		- Sensitivity - aware of followers needs and enviroment
