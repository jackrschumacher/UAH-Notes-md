  **Production and Productivity**  
- Production  
  – Transformation of organizational resources into products  
- Organizational Resources  
  – Assets available to generate profits  
- Transformation  
  – Steps necessary to change resources into products  
- Products  
  – Goods or services aimed at meeting human needs  
  
  **Productivity**  
- Relationship between total amount of goods or services being produced (output) and organizational resources needed to produce them (inputs)
- Productivity = Outputs/Inputs  
  
  **Strategies for Increasing Productivity**  
- Improving effectiveness of organizational workforce through training
- Improving production process through automation
- Improving product design to make products easier to assemble
- Improving production facility by purchasing more modern equipment
- Improving quality of workers hired to fill open positions  
  
  **Quality and Productivity**  
- Quality  
  – How well a product does what it is intended to do  
  – How closely product satisfies specifications to which it was built  
  – Degree of excellence on which products or services can be ranked on basis of selected features and characteristics  
  
  **Focus on Continual Improvement**  
- Improving product quality throughout all phases of production process
- Aim is to increase productivity of manufacturing system

  **Just-in-Time Inventory Control**  
- A technique for reducing inventories by arranging for production components to be delivered to the production facility “just-in-time”
- Also called “Zero Inventory” or “Kanban”
- Reduces labor costs, does not tie up money, and holds down inventory expenses  
  
  **Maintenance Control**  
- Keeps an organization’s facility and equipment functioning at predetermined work levels
- Pure-Preventive Policy: Action is taken before malfunctions
- Pure-Breakdown Policy: Action is taken only after malfunctions  
  
  **Cost Control**  
- Wide-ranging control aimed at keeping organizational costs at planned levels
- Stages in Cost Control  
  – Establishing standard cost amounts  
  – Measuring actual costs  
  – Comparing planned costs to actual costs  
  – Making changes to reduce actual costs to planned costs when necessary  
  
  **Budgetary Controls (1 of 2)**  
- Ensures that income and expenses occur as planned
- Budget: A single-use financial plan that covers a specified length of time
- Management Pitfalls of Budgets  
  – Placing too much emphasis on insignificant expenses  
  – Increasing expenses year after year without adequate information  
  – Ignoring that budgets must be changed periodically  
  
  **Budgetary Controls (2 of 2)**  
- Human Relations Considerations in Using Budgets  
  – Can create pressures that unite workers against management  
  – Can cause conflict between management and factory workers  
  – Can lead to worker inefficiency and aggression  
- Reducing Human Relations Problems  
  – Training on the formation and use of budgets  
  – Avoid using budgets to imply that workers are a success or a failure at their job
