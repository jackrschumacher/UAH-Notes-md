- **Chapter 12: Human Resource Management**  
  **Modern Management: Concepts & Skills, 15th Ed.**  
  
  **Learning Objectives**  
  By the end of class, you will be able to:  
	1. Explain the 4-step HRM process
	2. Describe how recruitment, selection, training, and appraisal work together
	3. Identify key employment laws
	4. Apply HRM concepts through hands-on activities  
  
**What Are "Appropriate" Human Resources?**

- Appropriate HR  
  – People who DO contribute to organizational goals  
- Inappropriate HR  
  – People who DO NOT contribute to organizational goals  
- The manager's job: find, develop, and keep the right people  
  
  **The 4-Step HRM Process**  
	1. Recruitment
	2. Selection
	3. Training
	4. Performance Appraisal  
  
**STEP 1: Recruitment**

- Attracting and screening prospective candidates  
  
  **Know the Job**  
- Job Analysis → Job Description + Job Specification  
  
  **Internal Sources**  
- Promotions, succession planning, morale boost  
  
  **External Sources**  
- Competitors, agencies, schools, publications
- Managers must stay current on recruiting trends  
  
  **Tools for Internal Recruitment**  
- Management inventory card
- Position replacement form
- Management manpower replacement chart  
  
  **Knowing the Laws**  
  You can't recruit or select without knowing these:  
- Civil Rights Act of 1964 (No discrimination by race, color, religion, sex, national origin)
- Pregnancy Discrimination Act
- Age Discrimination in Employment Act
- Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)
- Affirmative Action  
  
  **STEP 2: Selection**  
  Narrowing the candidate pool through a structured funnel:  
	1. Preliminary Screening (Records, data)
	2. Preliminary Interview
	3. Testing (Aptitude, achievement, personality, vocational interest)
	4. Diagnostic Interview
	5. Physical Exam + Personal Judgment  
  
**Key rules for tests:**

- Must be valid, reliable, non-discriminatory
- Never the sole basis for a hiring decision  
  
  **STEP 3: Training**  
- Training = developing employee qualities to increase productivity  
  
  **Training Process Steps**  
	1. Determine Training Needs
	2. Design the Training Program
	3. Administer the Program (Lectures, on-the-job, programmed learning, classroom)
	4. Evaluate Results  
  
**Evaluation questions:**

- Are rejection rates down?
- Are deadlines being met?
- Are labor costs decreasing?  
  
  **STEP 4: Performance Appraisal**  
- Reviewing past performance to evaluate contributions and guide improvement  
  
  **Why appraisals matter (Douglas McGregor):**  
- Support promotions, transfers, terminations
- Give employees feedback on how they're doing
- Coach and counsel for growth  
  
  **Common Methods**  
- Rating scales
- Employee comparisons
- Free-form essays
- Critical incident essays  
  
  **Multisource (360°) Feedback**  
- From supervisor, peers, subordinates, customers
- Used by 90% of Fortune 500 companies  
  
  **Performance Appraisal Pitfalls**  
- Focus only on short-term rewards
- Become a punishment tool
- Create excessive paperwork
- Are perceived as biased or unfair
- Trigger defensive reactions from employees  
  
  **Best practice:**  
- Focus on job performance
- Stay objective
- Make it a two-way conversation
