- ### Creativity
- The ability to generate original ideas or new perspectives on existing ideas
- For creative ideas to work, they must also be useful and actionable
- Creative organizations see issues from different angles and break away from old rules and norms
- ### Creativity in Individuals
- Function of three components:
	- **Expertise**: Work-related techniques and procedures
	- **Creative Thinking Skills**: Flexibility and imagination in approaching problems
	- **Motivation**: Individual’s need or passion to be creative
	  
	  **Figure 6.1**: The three components of creativity (Venn diagram showing overlap of Expertise, Creative Thinking Skills, and Motivation)  
- ### Increasing Creativity in Organizations
- Challenge workers
- Establish worker autonomy
- Afford time for accomplishing work
- Establish diverse work groups
- Personally encourage workers
- Establish systems support
- Hire and retain creative people

- ### Defining Innovation
- **Innovation**: The process of applying a new idea to the improvement of organizational processes, products, or services
- Organizations that do not innovate tend to become less competitive and attractive to customers
- Social networks and strong employee relations help foster innovation

- ### Linking Innovation and Creativity
- Ideas upon which innovation is based come from creativity in the organization
- Organizations that are creative but not innovative lack the ability to turn ideas into tangible benefits
  
  **Figure 6.4**: Creative Organization and Innovative Organization (Venn diagram illustrating overlap of creative and innovative organizations; creative organizations generate sound ideas for improvement; innovative organizations turn sound ideas into tangible benefit)  
- ### The Innovation Process
  **Figure 6.5**: How steps of the innovation process relate to Netflix (example flow: Inventing → Developing → Diffusing → Integrating → Monitoring)  
- **Step 1—Inventing**: Establishing a new idea
	- Categories: Technology, Product Ideas, Process Ideas, Management Ideas
- **Step 2—Developing**: Making a new idea practical
	- New ideas must be developed or made practical as a vehicle for enhancing organizational success
- **Step 3—Diffusing**: Putting new idea to use by end users/customers
	- Certain customers could be beta testers to test value and worth of the product
- **Step 4—Integrating**: Establishing invention as permanent part of organization
	- Making new process a standard operating procedure
- **Step 5—Monitoring**: Tracking of new idea to determine if and when idea should be improved or terminated
	- Management monitors newly implemented ideas to make sure that their contributions continue to accrue
- ### Catalyst for Creativity and Innovation: TQM
- **Quality**: How well a product does what it is supposed to do
- **Total Quality Management (TQM)**: Continuous process of involving all organizational members in ensuring every activity related to the production of goods or services has an appropriate role in establishing product quality
- ### Importance of Quality
- Positive company image
- Lower costs
- Higher market share
- Decreased product liability costs
  
  **Figure 6.6**: TQM typically results in greater market share and lower costs (flowchart showing Market Gains and Cost Savings paths leading to increased profits)  
- ### TQM Quality Awards
- **Deming Award**: Most prestigious international award; in honor of Edward Deming, who introduced TQM in Japan
- **Malcolm Baldrige National Quality Award**: Most widely known U.S. award
- ### The Incremental Improvement Process
- Step 1: Choose an area of improvement
- Step 2: Organize a quality improvement team
- Step 3: Identify benchmarks
- Step 4: Perform analysis of current performance
- Step 5: Perform a pilot study
- Step 6: Management implements the improvements
- ### Reengineering Improvements
  **Seven Reengineering Principles**:  
- Principle 1: Organize around outcomes, not tasks
- Principle 2: Those who use output perform the process
- Principle 3: Subsume information-processing work into real work
- Principle 4: Treat geographically dispersed resources as though they were centralized
- Principle 5: Link parallel activities instead of integrating their results
- Principle 6: Put decision point where work is performed
- Principle 7: Capture information once and at the source
