- ### Concept 16.1: DNA is the genetic material
	- Experiments with bacteria and with phages provided the first strong evidence that the genetic material is DNA
	- **Phages/Bacteriophage:** a virus that infects and replicates within bacteria
	- DNA is a a double helix, Two antiparallel sugar-phosphate chains on the outside, nitrogenous bases project into the interior
	- **Double Helix:** a pair of parallel helices intertwined about a common axis
- ### Concept 16.2: Many proteins work together in DNA replication and repair
	- DNA Replication is semiconservative
	- **DNA Replication:** Process by which a cell makes exact copies of its DNA
		- Occurs in all organsims
	- **Semiconservative replication:** Describes the mechanism of DNA replication in all known cells
	- **Replication fork:** Each of the newly divided daughter cell receives its own copy of each DNA molecule
	- ![55bb4e1ccd8b1e9d1a14781eaf0b201d.jpg](../assets/55bb4e1ccd8b1e9d1a14781eaf0b201d_1776564330471_0.jpg)
	- **DNA polymerases:** A member of a family of enzymes that catalyze the synthesis of DNA molecules from nucleoside triphosphates
	- **Mismatch repair:** A system for recognizing and repairing erroneous insertion, deletion, and mis-incorporation of bases that can arise during DNA replication and recombination
	- **Nucleotide excision repair:** DNA repair mechanism. Removes DNA damage from UV
	- **Nucleases:** An enzyme capable of cleaving the phosphodiester bonds that link nucleotides together to form nucleic acids
	- The ends of eukaryotic chromosomal DNA get shorter with each round of replication
	- **Telomeres:** A region of repetitive nucleotide sequences associated with specialized proteins at the ends of linear chromosomes
		- Most commonly found in eukaryotes
- ### Concept 16.3: Chromosome consists of a DNA molecule packed together with proteins
	- Chromosome of most bacterial species is a circular DNA molecule with some associated proteins, making up the nucleoid
	- Chromatin making up a eukaryotic chromosome is composed of DNA
	- **Chromatin:** the readily stainable substance of a cell nucleus consisting of DNA and RNA and various proteins; during mitotic division it condenses into chromosomes
	- Histones bind to each other and to the DNA to form nucleosomes
	- **Nucleosomes:** Segment of DNA wound around eight histone proteins
	- Histone tails extend outward from each bead-like nucleosome core
	- Chromosomes occupy restricted areas in the interphase nucleus
	- **Euchromatin:** Lightly packed for of chromatin that is enriched in genes, and is often under active transcription
		- 92% of the human genome is euchromatic
	- **Heterochromatin:** tightly packed chromatin
	- Euchromatin, but not heterochromatin, is generally accessible for gene transcription
