    Module 1 (Gilgamesh)

    • **

      General Notes

    • Stories date back as far as 2100

    • 1700 BCE

    • 600 standard version- Babylonian

    • Believed that the epic may have been a school text

    • Text was first deciphered in mid 1800’s

    • Key form script

      Tablet I

    • Toys with the people of the earth

      • The king is Brutal- his subjects complain to Ishtar

    • Temple in Rome

    • Ishtar complains to the higher up gods

    • Spy for Gilgamesh tames the beast through sex

    • Mortal man reaches for divine immortality

    • Gilgamesh is alone

      Tablet II

    • Adventure

    • Gilgamesh wants eternal fame

      • Wants to be talked about even in death
      • Demi god
      • Toys with subjects

    • What if Gilgamesh is vulnerable to death?

      • Awakened to own Mortality

        Tablet III

    • Gilgamesh goes to his mothers temple to pray

    • Mother asks the gods about her sons divinity

    • Gilgamesh wants to be a god not just like a god

      Tablet IV

    • Group tires to make a trip to Mount Lebanon

    • Traveled a months journey in a single day

    • Gilgamesh’s dreams

      • Mountain (Humbaba) falls upon the party

      • Saw Samash the king

      • The heavens cried out, the earth was thundering, Daylight faded, darkness fell

      • Lion-headed monster bird Anzu in the sky

      • I was grasping a wild bull of the steppe

        Tablet V

    • They stood there, marveling at the forest, they gazed at the height of the cedars

    • They beheld the cedar mountain, dwelling of the gods

    • Gilgamesh does not want his physical body to fail

    • Humbamba is the king of his kingdom

    • City vs Wilderness

    • Man v Nature

    • Life and Death

    • Forest- Wild, uncultivated waste, wilderness

      • In this case the forest that Gilgamesh encounters may be set aside by a king for hunting

    • Gilgamesh has become a sort of outlaw

    • Gilgamesh is beset by fear

    • Gilgamesh forced his way into the forest- dwelling of the gods (forest appears to be similar to a wasteland)

      Tablet VI

    • now thinks that his status is equal to the deity of his city

    • Gilgamesh will be destroyed

      Tablet VII

    • Enkidu raging about his impending mortality- prophecy of his own death

    • Shamash unhappy with Enkudis issue- does not believe that he should be dwelling on that

    • Enkidu also has a vision of the underworld- related to gilgamesh- foreshadows

    • Enkidu falls ill and dies 12 days later - Gilgamesh grieves for his friend

      Tablet VIII

    • Gilgamesh kind of comes off the rails

      • Paces around, throws his clothes off, etc. - puts on the lions skin

    • Dams the Euphrates river in order to build a tomb for his friend

    • Runs into the wilderness in order to run from his grief and also to escape his own mortality

      • Wonders if he must die too

        Tablet IX

    • Struggles with his own mortality

      • “Shall I not die too?”
      • Roams the steppe to get away

    • Prays to the Moon god to grant him a vision- Travels to the underworld and meets the scorpions guarding the gates of the underworld

      • Gilgamesh is the first to visit this mountain
      • Scorpion tells Gilgamesh that he can use a tunnel to travel to Utnapishtim even though it is believed that no mortal could survive the journey

    • The journey is undertaken in total darkness

    • Journey ends in a lush garden and a sea

          Tablet 10

    • Gilgamesh encounters Siduri, a veiled bridesmaid at the edge of the aforementioned sea.

      • Tells Gilgamesh that only the gods are truly immortal

    • Tells Gilgamesh that Samash (sun god) crosses the sea, but no mortal has ever been able to follow him

    • Tells him to ask Urshanabi to take him to Utnapishtim

      • Attacks stone things and snakes - makes it more difficult to get there because they protect and propel the boat
      • Introduces himself
      • Gilgamesh must cut poles in order to build a raft to complete the voyage

    • 3 day voyage

    • Meets an old man that says that death is destiny decided by the gods

          Final Tablets

    • Old man is Utnapishtim

      • Ea leaks gods plans for a flood to Utnapishtim

    • Is told to build a boat with the seeds of each living thing and family etc.

      • Essentially the story of Noah’s ark
      • Made them gods in order to make up for destroying the world and much of the human population

    • Tells Gilgamesh about a plant that makes one younger

    • Gilgamesh meets with the gods and Ea allows Enkidu and Gilgamesh visit

      • The dead who are worst off in the underworld are those who have not left behind people to mourn them
