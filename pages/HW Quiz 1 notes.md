- **Hedger:** an investor or company that uses strategies and financial instruments  to reduce or offset a risk
- **Information Motivated Trader:** Believe that they have superior information compared to others
- **Investor:** Person or organization that puts money into financial plans,etc with the expectation of achieving a profit
- **YOLO trader:** Trader that engages in high risk, speculative stock market behavior
- **Derivative investment market:** Global financial marketplace for contracts that derive their value from an underlying asset such as a stock, bond or commodity
- **Alternative investment market:** Asset classes outside of traditional stocks and bonds
```
    An investor primarily invests in stocks of publicly traded companies. The investor wants to increase the diversification of his portfolio. A friend has recommended investing in real estate properties. The purchase of real estate would best be characterized as an alternative investment strategy.
```
- **Money market instruments:** Short-term, low-risk and highly liquid debt instruments
```
    A hedge fund holds its excess cash in 90-day commercial paper and negotiable certificates of deposit. The cash management policy of the hedge fund is best described as money market instruments
```
- **Primary market:** Where new securities are sold for the first time
```
    An oil and gas exploration and production company announces that it is offering 30 million shares to the public at $45.50 each. 
```
- **Secondary market:** Investors trade existing shares with each other on a stock exchange
```
    The Standard & Poor’s Depositary Receipts (SPDRs) is an investment that tracks the S&P 500 stock market index. Purchases and sales of SPDRs during an average trading day are best described as: secondary market transactions in a pooled investment
```
- **Call option:** Gives the buyer the right to purchase an asset at a specific price, betting the price will rise
- **Put option:** gives the buyer the right to sell an asset at a specific price betting that the stock will fall
- | Feature  | Call Option | Put Option |
    | **Buyer's Expectation** | The underlying asset's price will go up (bullish) | The underlying asset's price will go down (bearish) |
    | **Buyer's Right** | To **buy** the asset at the strike price | To **sell** the asset at the strike price |
    | **Profit Potential** | Limited risk (premium paid), potentially unlimited profit | Limited risk (premium paid), limited profit (limited to the strike price) |
    | **Maximum Loss** | The premium paid for the option | The premium paid for the option |
    | **Risk** | If the price doesn't rise above the strike price by expiration, the option expires worthless and the buyer loses the premium | If the price doesn't fall below the strike price by expiration, the option expires worthless and the buyer loses the premium |
- **Private placement:** company sells securities to a select group of investors rather than to the general public
```
    A British company listed on AIM (formerly the Alternative Investment Market)
    of the London Stock Exchange announced the sale of 6,686,665 shares to a small
    group of qualified investors at £0.025 per share.
```
- **Rights offering:** A corporate action that gives existing shareholders the opportunity but not the obligation to purchase additional shares of the company's stock at a discounted price
```
    A German publicly traded company, to raise new capital, gave its existing shareholders the opportunity to subscribe for new shares. The existing shareholders could purchase two new shares at a subscription price of €4.58 per share for every 15 shares held. 
```
- **Liquid market:** A financial or commercial market with a high volume of activity, where assets can be bought or sold quickly without causing a significant change in price.
- **High Trading Volume**: There are a large number of ready and willing buyers and sellers at any given time.
- **Low Transaction Costs**: The costs associated with buying and selling (e.g., the bid-ask spread) are typically low due to the high volume of participants.
- **Quick and Efficient Transactions**: Trades can be executed rapidly, allowing investors to convert assets into cash quickly.
- **Price Stability (Resilience)**: Individual large transactions have minimal impact on the asset's market price.
```
    A financial analyst is examining whether a country’s financial market is well functioning. She finds that the transaction costs in this market are low and trading volumes are high. She concludes that the market is quite liquid. In such a market: traders will find it easy to trade and their trading will make the market more informationally efficient
```
